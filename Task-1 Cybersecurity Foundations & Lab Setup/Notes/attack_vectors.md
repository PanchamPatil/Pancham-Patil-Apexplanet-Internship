# Attack Vectors

## What is an Attack Vector?
An attack vector is the method or pathway used by attackers to gain unauthorized access to a system or network.

---

## 1. Social Engineering
Manipulating human behavior to gain confidential information.

### Examples:
- Phishing emails
- Fake phone calls
- Impersonation

---

## 2. Wireless Attacks
Exploiting vulnerabilities in wireless networks.

### Examples:
- Evil Twin attack
- Wi-Fi password cracking
- Packet sniffing

---

## 3. Insider Threats
Threats originating from within the organization.

### Causes:
- Malicious intent
- Human error
- Lack of awareness

---

## 4. Software Vulnerabilities
Exploiting unpatched or misconfigured software.

### Examples:
- Outdated systems
- Weak passwords
- Default credentials

---

## 5. Physical Attacks
Gaining access through physical means.

### Examples:
- USB drops
- Unauthorized access to devices

---

## Conclusion
Attack vectors highlight how attackers enter systems. Understanding them helps in building stronger defense mechanisms.
